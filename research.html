Working Papers

Mutual Fund Competition and the Price Impact of Fire Sales

Abstract: This paper investigates the effect of mutual fund competition on the price impact of fire sales. Stocks sold in high-competition segments experience larger negative price impacts, followed by price reversals that provide greater liquidity compensation. We examine two mechanisms. First, illiquid stocks in high-competition segments are more likely to be sold under outflow pressure, and such transactions receive less liquidity support. Second, slower information diffusion in low-competition segments allows funds to sell stocks with passive information at smaller discounts. As a result, competition drives funds in high-competition segments to sell stocks more closely aligned with their fundamental quality.


Beta Uncertainty, Disagreement, and Mutual Fund Investor Learning

Abstract: This paper documents a negative relationship between beta uncertainty and the sensitivity of mutual fund flows to past performance. We interpret beta uncertainty as capturing disagreement among investors arising from heterogeneous prior beliefs about the benchmark used to evaluate managerial skill. Within a Bayesian learning framework, such disagreement increases noise in performance signals, weakens posterior belief updating, and limits investors’ inference about skill from realized returns. Consistent with these predictions, we empirically show that higher beta uncertainty significantly lowers flow-performance sensitivity. This effect is stronger when investors’ priors are more heterogeneous and is attributable to disagreement rather than ambiguity aversion. Learning from funds with lower beta uncertainty is more informative, thereby strengthening investors’ incentive to learn.
